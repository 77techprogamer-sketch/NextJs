## Term Life Insurance: The Complete Guide for Indian Families

Term life insurance is the simplest and most affordable form of life insurance. You pay a premium for a fixed period, and if you die during that term, your family receives a lump sum. If you survive, you get nothing — unless you opt for return of premium.

Think of it like car insurance — you hope you never need it, but if something happens, your family is protected.

---

## What Exactly is Term Life Insurance?

Term life insurance provides a death benefit to your nominees if you pass away during the policy term. It does not build cash value, does not pay dividends, and does not offer any return if you survive the term.

This simplicity is its strength: for every ₹1,000 of annual premium, you can get ₹1 crore of cover. Compare this to endowment plans where ₹1,000 gets you only ₹5-7 lakh cover.

---

## Why Do You Need Term Life Insurance?

If anyone depends on your income — spouse, children, aging parents — you need term life insurance.

**Replace your income:** If you die, your family loses your income. Term insurance gives them a lump sum that can generate income to replace your salary for decades.

**Pay off debts:** Home loan, car loan, personal loan — all become your family's burden after your death. ₹1 crore cover can clear all debts and leave money for living.

**Fund children's education:** A child's education costs ₹20-50 lakh today. With inflation, it will be ₹1 crore+ in 20 years.

**Cover family expenses:** Monthly household expenses × 12 × years until youngest child is independent = your minimum cover.

---

## How Much Life Cover Do You Need?

Use the Human Life Value formula:

**Sum Assured = (Annual Income × Years Left) + Debts + Future Expenses − Savings**

Example: ₹10L/year, 25 years left, ₹40L home loan, ₹30L education, ₹10L savings = ₹3 Crore

Quick rule: 15-20x annual income for ages 25-35, 10-15x for ages 35-45, 5-10x for ages 45-55.

## When Should You Buy Term Insurance?

**As early as possible.** Here is why:

**Lower premium:** ₹1 crore at age 25 = ₹7,000/year. At age 40 = ₹18,000/year. That is ₹2.2 lakh extra over 15 years for waiting.

**Better health:** Younger = healthier = lower premium. If you develop diabetes or hypertension later, premium increases 25-50% or cover gets rejected.

**Longer coverage:** Buy at 25, cover till 55 = 30 years. Buy at 40, cover till 55 = only 15 years.

**Tax saving starts early:** Section 80C deduction of ₹1.5 lakh/year from day one.

## Common Myths About Term Insurance (Debunked)

**Myth 1: "I am young, I do not need insurance"**
Reality: Your parents/spouse will have to pay your debts and funeral costs. Plus, buying young locks in low premiums forever.

**Myth 2: "LIC is the best because it is government"**
Reality: LIC has good CSR but private insurers often have lower premiums, better features, and faster service. Compare all options.

**Myth 3: "Endowment plans give money back, term is waste"**
Reality: Endowment gives 4-5% return. Term + invest difference in mutual funds gives 10-12% return with 15x more cover.

**Myth 4: "I have employer insurance, that is enough"**
Reality: Employer insurance ends when you leave the job. What if you leave and then die? Always have personal cover.

**Myth 5: "Smokers cannot get insurance"**
Reality: Smokers CAN get insurance. Premium is 25-50% higher but cover is available. Do not hide smoking — claim will be rejected.

4. **Fill application:** Personal details, health history, lifestyle (smoking, drinking), nominee details.

7. **Set up auto-pay:** Do not let policy lapse due to missed payment. Opt for annual payment (cheaper than monthly).

## Important Riders to Add

**Accidental Death Benefit:** Extra sum assured if death is due to accident. Adds ₹50-100 to premium per lakh.

**Critical Illness Rider:** Lump sum on diagnosis of cancer, heart attack, stroke, etc. Use instead of separate CI plan.

**Waiver of Premium:** If you become disabled, future premiums are waived but cover continues. Highly recommended.

**Income Benefit:** Monthly income to family (10-20% of sum assured) for 10-15 years after death.

---

## Tax Benefits

- **Section 80C:** Premium up to ₹1.5 lakh/year is deductible
- **Section 10(10D):** Death benefit received by nominee is completely tax-free
- **Condition:** Premium must be ≤ 10% of sum assured for tax-free maturity
